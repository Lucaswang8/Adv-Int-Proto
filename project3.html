Project 3 : Detecting Uninvited Guests

A data visualisation project that detects uninvited guests in my living room.

Week 1 : Ideation

Initial Diagram

Inspiration

I have friends who I gave the keys to my house to. Sometimes they show up uninvited. They usually stays in my living room downstairs while I stay upstairs. But times I live alone, I hear sounds and I am unsure if they are there or am I tripping. So I've always wanted to check if they are there without me needing to leave the safety of my room.

Hence, I thought why not use sensors like Infrared to check whether they are there. Using motion sensor to detect if they are human instead of just objects blocking. Microphone (which I can't find on tinkerCAD) to detect them talking. Photocell to detect if they turnt on the lights.

Week 2 : Making Sensors

Week 3 : Final Outcome

Changing the System to be simpler

For week 3, I started off by changing the code to be simpler. I just wanted to draw paint drips. The pots will determine the coordinate and the button will draw it. However, the x coordinate seems to not be working. Only the y coordinate does. I tried to fix it but it doesn't work despite multiple unrecorded tries. This is where I end up with. I think in the future, I'll start by testing if the pots and the coordinates work before I code the entire interaction.

Final Outcome

Reading 3 : The Internet of Things

They are willing to do whatever it takes to reengineer the body so it gets more done in less time, is easier and more pleasant to work with—to render themselves, as the old Radiohead lyrics put it, “calm, fitter, healthier and more productive,” and in so doing transform themselves into all-but-fungible production units, valued only in terms of what they offer the economy

This quote hits me really hard as I find myself constantly needing to be productive hence I try to take control of every aspect of my life. Yet the constant thought that I could be better was knawing on my self esteem. Yet somehow the action I'm taking to be more productive give me a sort of ego boost at the same time. Reading this made me realise what I'm essentially doing to myself and needing to come in terms with this fact made me also feel less tough on myself as I start to open my eyes towards things outside of my productive output to the economy.

the parties that understand the vulnerabilities— device manufacturers—aren’t incentivized to fix them, while the end user doesn’t have the expertise to do so

After learning about how internet of things could be exploited, this quote made me realise why this problem persist in our society. Not gonna lie, it made me want to lose all hope in humanity but I feel like there might be a way out of this if we continue questioning and raising our expectations for these devices to be more responsible.

If you give me six lines written by the hand of the most honest of men, I will find something in them with which to hang him.

This quote make me realise how important data is and how it can be used maliciously once it is in the wrong hands. We can't guarantee whoever taking over our data isn't malicious in the future. Thus, we should start to worry the initial act of collecting data and question the purpose of it.
